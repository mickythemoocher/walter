THE UNFORTUNATE MR RALEIGH

Characters

Sir Walter Raleigh

Queen Elizabeth I

Kate (The Queens Lady In Waiting)

Bert (Sir Walter's friend and companion)

William (The queen's advisor)

The scene is The Court Of Elizabeth I.

Kate: Pardon me Ma'am but Sir Walter awaits you.

Queen: Already? I wasn't expecting him until tomorrow.

Kate: He seems full of himself as usual.

Queen: Oooh, perhaps he has brought me something exciting from his travels.

Kate: I wish he had got lost on his travels.

Queen: Oh come on now Kate, don't be mean about him.

Kate: I think he is a stuck up, pompous ass.

Queen: I don't think so at all, I think he is quite dishy.

Kate: And he smells.

Queen: That's not his fault, deodorant hasn't been invented yet.

Kate: He could at least have a wash occasionally.

Queen: He has been at sea for two years. He's bound to whiff a bit. Anyway, you had better show him in.

Kate:Alright then. (loudly) Oi! Walt, you can come in now. And bring that smelly mate of yours with you.

( After a short pause)

Walter: Ahh You Majesty, It is such a delight to cast my eyes upon you once again.

Queen (very quietly) You're right Kate, he does whiff a bit. (normal voice) Sir Walter it is good to see you again and that you have returned safely from you voyage.

Walter: It was a hazardous trip Ma'am but with the grace of God we have arrived home safely. Towards the end of the journey we had little food, little water and little hope.

Kate: Little soap too I should imagine Walt

Queen. (quietly) Shusss Kate.

Walter: Alas dear lady, we had not of that at all.

Kate: Oh what a surprise. I would have been mortified without soap. I simply have to have a bath every six weeks if I need it or not.

Queen: And who is this gallant young man with you Walter?

Walter: This is my very good friend and companion Bert.

Queen: Pleased to meet you Bert.

Bert: Nice to meet you too Miss

Walter: (in a whisper) Idiot, it's Ma'am, Ma'am not Miss

Bert: Begging your pardon Ma'am Ma'am

Walter: (in a whisper) Just one Ma'am not two.

Bert: You said Ma'am Ma'am.

Walter: Oh you fool, I was just trying to make it clear to you.

Bert: Saying it twice didn't make it seem very clear.

Walter: Please excuse Bert Ma'am, he has been at sea a long time.

Queen: Possibly not long enough.

Walter: A thousand apologies Your Majesty

Queen: That is a very nice head you have, Bert

Bert: Thank you very much Miss, err sorry, I mean Ma'am Ma'am. Oh no, sorry just one Ma'am. Thank you.

Queen: Looks perfectly balanced on your shoulders at the moment, it would be a shame to dislodge it.

Kate: Ooooh I love watching a good beheading.

Queen: So, Walter have you brought me back some wondrous gifts from you long travels?

Walter: I have indeed Ma'am.

Queen: Excellent, that is very exciting. Before you show me though I must have William here.

Walter: Your personal advisor Ma'am?

Queen: Yes, mostly he advises me on who's head to chop off.

Bert: Not mine I hope.

Queen: Fingers crossed, eh Bert?

Bert: Yes mi...... er.... Ma'am.

Queen: Kate would you ask William to join us.

Kate: I suppose so. (loudly) Oi William. Come in here.

Walter: It will be a pleasure to see him again Ma'am.

Queen: You know he doesn't like you Walter?

Bert: A lot of people don't like walter. In fact I am his only friend.

Walter: Shut up Bert. - I am quite aware Ma'am that he does not like me.

Queen: In fact Walter, he detests you ( a little laugh). He keeps saying I should chop your head off.

Walter: That would be unfortunate.

Kate: Not for me.

Queen: So really Walter it won't really be a pleasure to see him again will it?

Walter: I suppose not.

Queen: Ah, William please come and join us.

William: Delighted to You Majesty

Queen: Walter has bought back some gifts for me and I wanted your opinion of them.

William: Which I would be delighted to do.

Queen: So what have you got to show me? And William, don't look at Walters head like that

William: I beg your pardon Ma'am. I was just wondering how heavy it is.

Queen: No need for that right at the moment. Walter?

Walter: Bert, please take the first gift from the bag.

Bert: This is my favourite one.

Walter: Pass it carefully to me.

Bert: Here we are mate.

Queen: (loudly) Oh my goodness! What on Earth is that?

Bert: Not the reaction we were expecting was it.

Walter: Shhhh Bert.

Queen: I do believe that is the ugliest thing I have ever seen. What's it called

Walter: It is called Ma'am - A potato.

Bert: Is it? That's news to me.

William: I believe he just made that up.

Kate: (quietly) I might buy a new hat for the beheading.

Queen: It is ugly, dirty, full of sprouty bits and if I am not mistaken, covered in mud.

Bert: Can't argue with that can we?

Queen: William, why are you smiling?

William: Sorry Ma'am, no reason, no reason.

Queen: So Walter. What are we supposed to do with that disgusting looking article.

Walter: Well Ma'am, err, Actually, errr. Well the thing is if your Majesty agrees, and I quite understand if she doesn't.....

Bert: We should eat it.

Queen: (very loudly) EAT IT!!

(William and kate laugh softly)

Walter: Yes, hmm eat it. That's correct.

Queen: Walter, have you lost your mind? Do you think any sane person in this green and pleasant land will ever in their wildest dreams want to eat - that!

Bert: It's very popular in Spain.

William: So is the inquisition but we don't want it here.

Walter: It really is quite delicious Ma'am

Bert: With plenty of salt, maybe some pepper, a smidge of mustard perhaps. Need something else with it to give it a bit of flavour, some beef perhaps, with some gravy added. Doesn't taste too bad then.

Walter: Will you shut up Bert.

William: It certainly seems like something that would never pass my lips.

Kate: I'm not sure it will ever pass Walts either ( a little laugh)

Queen: Walter, I am not happy with that gift. I was expecting gold or a diamond, something like that. It would be fair to say that I find a potato a gross disappointment.

Kate: I would say that is an understatement.

William: I would say I need to sharpen my ax.

Queen: Shhh William, We haven't got to that stage yet.

Walter: I regret your disappointment. I had all good intentions of finding something that would feed the nation.

Kate: You'd be lucky to feed pigs with that.

Queen: So Walter, what else do you have? I hope you will not disappoint me again.

Kate: I rather hope he does.

William:I can't wait to see what he has next.

Walter: My next gift Ma'am comes from far, far, far away from a land they call the Americas. While I was there, a small corner of this land your majesty, I named after you.

Queen: Oh Walter, that is so very sweet indeed of you. Thank you.

Kate: Crawler.

William: Biding his time.

Queen: So there is now a land far, far away called Elizabeth?

Walter: Err, well no, not exactly Ma'am. I named it Virginia?

Queen: (surprised) Virginia? But my name is Elizabeth.

Walter: Well, yessssss but you know, you are a …. Oh never mind, I'll change it when I go back.

William: I really think you better had.

Queen: So what is this wondrous gift from the land that was very nearly named after me?

Walter: The other gift please Bert.

Bert: Just a minute, it's right down the bottom of the sack.

Walter: Get it out gently, I don't want it to break it up

Bert: Here you are then.

Walter. Ok, I have it.

Queen: I am waiting Walter:

Walter: Waiting Ma'am?

Queen: Yes Walter, I am waiting to see my gift.

Walter: Ahh – Your gift Ma'am?

Kate: Yes Walt she is waiting for the gift you promised.

William: I do hope for your sake, and your heads, that it is a good one.

Walter: Well, yes, yes of course..

Queen: So where is it.

Bert: Hmm I think this might be the point where I leave.

Walter: You stay right here. This was your idea as much as mine

Bert: No it wasn't!

Walter: Yes it was.

Queen: Boys, boys, will you please stop arguing amongst yourselves and show me my gift.

Walter: (clears throat) Hmm well the thing is you Majesty, this is your gift.

Queen: This?

Walter: Yes Ma'am, this.

William: Oh dear.

Kate: Why are you smiling William?

William: No reason. It must be wind.

Queen: I repeat myself. This?

Walter: Yes Ma'am, this.

Queen: Do you mean Bert? Is he my gift?

Bert: No, no, no not me. I'm not a gift

Walter: This Ma'am. This that I am holding in my hand?

Queen: In your hand?

Walter: Errrr, yes. In my hand.

Queen: But Walter, in your hand you have a.... well you have a leaf!

Walter: Yes Ma'am.

Kate: Not even a pretty one, it's all brown and wrinkled.

Queen: Almost uglier than the potato.

Walter: But your Majesty, this is indeed a rather special leaf.

Queen: (sarcastically) Ohh I see. A special leaf.

Kate: Is it magic then?

William: It had better be.

Walter: Not magic as such.

Bert: I am beginning to feel very uncomfortable

William: I think that is a wise thing to feel Bert.

Walter: But it is not just an ordinary leaf it has special qualities

Queen: So tell me Walter, what is the name of this leaf that has such special qualities.

Walter: It is called tobacco ma'am.

Queen: And it comes from a land called Elizabeth? Oh no not Elizabeth, Virginia?

Walter: Yes Ma'am it does.

Queen: So pray tell me Walter, what do we do with this – tobacco.

William: This better be good.

Kate: Very good.

Walter: Firstly it needs to be shredded into very small pieces.

Queen: I see, very interesting. Then what happens to my wonderful present?

Walter: The next step is we roll the shreds into a small piece of paper.

Queen: How exciting, I can hardly wait.

Bert: I could do with rolling one right now.

William: I have a feeling it won't be the only thing rolling.

Kate: I get that feeling too, rather strongly.

Queen: So now we have the tiny shreds rolled in paper. What next?

Bert: This is the exciting bit.

Walter: Next we, well we err.. Next we burn it?

Queen: (sarcastically again) Oh what fun. I can hardly wait to try it.

Walter: Then so you shall your Majesty.

Queen: ( very loudly) I was joking Walter!

Walter: Oh I see, sorry

William: You will be.

Kate: Not a good sign. When she has to tell you she is joking.

Walter: There is more Ma'am.

Queen: You mean more presents?

Walter: No, I mean more about this one.

Queen: Then do tell.

Walter: Once it is burning – well, hmm, you put it in your mouth.

William: ( whistle quietly knowing it is going bad for Walter)

Queen: (laughs). I am sorry Walter. I misheard you. I thought you said that once it is burning you put it in your mouth.

Walter: I, err, Yes, I did say that Ma'am.

Queen: William, pray tell me what you think of these gifts that Walter has brought to me?

William: In all honesty Ma'am. I believe they are an insult to you.

Kate: I was thinking the very same thing.

Queen: I didn't ask you Kate.

Kate: Ooops, sorry.

Queen: So what do we do with people that insult the queen William?

William: Quite often Ma'am – we chop off their heads

Queen: Yes indeed, quite often we chop of their heads. Did you hear that Walter.

Walter( Big gulp) Yes I did Ma'am.

Queen: Tell me then, you say this leaf has special qualities. So once we have shredded it, rolled it in paper, set fire to it then put it in our mouths, what is the special quality?

Bert: Oh dear.

Walter: To put it bluntly Ma'am, not wanting to beat about the bush, Telling it like it is, being open and frank......

Queen: Get on with it man – what does it do?

Walter: Errr... It makes us cough

(Quite a long pause)

William: I'll just go see if my ax has been sharpened....

THE END
